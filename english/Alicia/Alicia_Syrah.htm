Our wines :: Viña Alicia :: Syrah
                
   

Syrah

This is a variety that requires special knowledge of viticulture. The plants in our vineyard were selected folowing years of detailed monitoring and study to thus establish their quality.

 

Location

 

It is found in Luján de  Cuyo, 18 km south east of Mendoza city, 900 m above sea level.

 

Climate

 

The autumn is warm and winter, intensely cold. Summer days are temperate and its nights quite cool. These conditions are ideal for reaching good maturity and wines of great body.

Temperature averages are, for summer, 23°C and for winter, -4 to -5°C. Rainfall is 220 mm per year.

 

Soil

 

The origin of these soils is alluvial, with layered strata of chalk, limestone and clay, conditions that allow stability and permeable qualities, assisting root health and oxygen exchange.

Soil fertility es closely checked, by means of routine tests, to verify the required balance.

 

Vineyard Description

 

This is a 10 hectare vineyard. The average age of vines is 20 years. Yields are kept low to enable each plant to reach its full potencial.

Carried out as naturally as possible, organically, dispensing with use of fertilizers, herbicides and even insecticides. If any, only natural products are used. Biological pest control and surface irrigation, using mountain water, rich in minerals and sediments, complete the picture.

Double Guyot system is followed. Additional practices ensure that the maximum yield allowance of 40  hectoliters per hectare is observed.

 

Harvest

This happens in March, is carried out manually, using small 12 to 15 kilo plastic cages, to protect against grape damage and contamination.

As in all the other varieties, at this point two previous bunch selections have taken place. One just after flowering and the second, when the grapes have acquired color, to eliminate malformed, defective  or insufficiently colored bunches.

  

Stage in Winery

 

When the grapes arrive at the winery, the third bunch selection takes place. It is performed with scissors, over a conveyor belt, to discard unripe, dry or withered berries, just before stem removal. This procedure is done by a machine placed directly over the mouth of the fermentation vessel.

Fermentation and maceration of this variety takes place at 25-28°C, with the objective of prolonging the contact of grape skin with the juices. This requires a period of 30 days.

"Pumping up" and "Punching down" procedures are performed daily.

 

Storage ageing time

 

Once fermentation is completed, it is poured into new American oak casks for 3-5 months. After this stage, it is left in French oak casks for a period of 8 to 10 months before clarification is performd with egg white.

 

Minimum bottle stowage

 

Ageing in bottles enables wine to express its full potential, evidencing virtues hitherto unexpressed. The time spent in bottles gives quality and refinement. This wine is bottled without previous filtering.

The period of repose in bottles lasts 10 months, before it is considered ready for marketing.

This is a variety that rewards the connoisseur that acquieres it.

 

Organoleptic characteristics

 

This is a wine that is mild, perfumed, rounded and warm. There is a depth to it, with gentle, abundant tannins. The two hour wait it takes for it to elicit its smoky and mineral aroma, with cocoa, mulberry, spice and vanilla features, is well worthwhile. The ripeness of  the grape has left its heritage of complexity.

 

Gourmet Notes

 Its delicate nature makes it ideal for accompanying lean fish, duck, grilled pork and pastas.
