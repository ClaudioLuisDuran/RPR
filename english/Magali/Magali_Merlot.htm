Merlot

 

This purplish red Vinos Magali Merlot tastes of ripe red fruit, dry leaves, cherries, plums and marmalade. It ages with a distinct flavor of truffles that makes it stand out from other Merlot wines.

 

LOCATION OF VINEYARD

The grapes for our three premium red wines were grown in Finca (vineyard) Agrelo located in Luján de Cuyo, in the Province of Mendoza, in the West of Argentina, 10 miles to the Southwest of the city of Mendoza at 2,750 feet (850 meters) above sea level. The region is at the foot of the Cordillera de los Andes and the pearl of the Andes, the Aconcagua.

CLIMATE

Luján de Cuyo, with its cold winters, and warm, rainless summers provides the perfect conditions to give our grapes exact ripeness.

SOIL

Even in Ancient Rome it was said Bacchus amat colles, or Bacchus loves the hills, suggesting that hillside vineyards have long been regarded as a source of high quality wines.

Our Finca Agrelo rests on eastern slopes less prone to frost because cold air can drain freely away at night. The holistic combination of our permeable, limy, sandy soil, with our topography, result in a perfect terroir that is enhanced by the rich mineral waters of the lands of the Aconcagua.

These waters run freely through channels and trenches that are used to provide ideal irrigation to the vineyards.

VINEYARD DESCRIPTION

The Finca "Agrelo" Vineyard has been distinguished as one of the best locations for growing red grapes. It is a 13-yearold vineyard meticulously cared for by expert cultivators.

Finca Agrelo yields a production of 60 quintals per hectare for the cabernet, 60 quintals per hectare for the malbec and 60 quintals per hectare for the merlot. These low yields allow the vines to reach full ripeness, thus improving wine quality. The hillside resting vines are grown on espaliers which support vigorous growth, enhance the exposure to sunlight and prevent disease.

The merlot vines are grown on a low espalier with 5000 vines per hectare, all trained to a modified double Guyot system.

HARVESTING METHOD

Our grapes are hand picked by trained pickers. This method as opposed to mechanical harvesting, allows pickers to select individual bunches according to their ripeness and to eliminate unhealthy fruit. The fruit is gently handled and put into small containers since the shallower the depth of fruit, the less damage it will suffer.

WINE MAKING

Our wines undergo fermentation in two large vats. The first is used exclusively for primary fermentation. The second one is for secondary fermentation of new wines and the beginning of their conservation. Both fermentation and maceration are performed in stainless steel tanks at controlled temperature. After this step, the wines receive a careful soutirage and pressing during the first week, and they are then transferred to premium French oak barrels to complete the élevage. In the last step, the wines are clarified with egg whites. Once bottled, they are allowed to rest for at least 90 days to finish the process

BOTTLING

We subject the wines to minimal aeration and agitation and bottles are filled slowly so that there is no splashing or foaming. The entire process is stress free to protect the outstanding features of the wines. We use the highest quality corks as stoppers to avoid contamination and harmful contact with oxygen.

STORAGE AND TRANSPORTATION

Our wines are stored in controlled temperature and humidity environments to preserve their quality characteristics. Likewise, our wines are transported in thermally insulated containers to protect against temperature variations.
